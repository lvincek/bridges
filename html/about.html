Board of Directors

Rick Castro, President & Co-Founder

Rick co-founded Bridges to Manila in 2008 and has promoted BTM’s mission and vision for over 9 years. Rick’s inspiration for Bridges to Manila came both from his father, a Filipino physician from Calinog, Iloilo (where Western Visayas State University is located) and his trips to the Philippines since 2006. In that trip he visited Filipino schools, met with faculty, students and their families and saw the dire need for outside financial help to bolster the education in the community.

Rick is currently a Regional Sales Director for Invoca in San Francisco, where he has worked for 2 years. He’s a 6 year veteran of Google and has worked in the Internet industry for over 18 years. Rick has a BS in Business from Miami of Ohio and an MBA from Carnegie Mellon.

Mark Castro, Founder and Director

Mark has assisted Rick with Bridges to Manila since 2008 and shares in Rick’s passion and vision of the foundation. He has accompanied Rick on his trips to the Philippines in 2006 and most recently with his own family in 2017.

Mark works as a Senior Environmental Project Manager for Commonwealth Edison, a power utility company located in Chicago, Illinois. Prior to that, he had worked in environmental consulting for over 15 years. Mark obtained a BS in Biology from the University of Dayton and a MSPH in Environmental Health Sciences from the University of South Carolina. Mark resides in Brookfield, a suburb outside of Chicago, with his wife Kate and their three children Ray (8), Myles (7) and Blake (5).

Matt Castro, Founder and Director

Matt has assisted Rick with Bridges to Manila since 2008 and shares in Rick’s passion and vision of the foundation. He has accompanied Rick on his trips to the Philippines as well.

Matt works as a Senior Manager in Service Delivery at LUMEN and has been at the same company for 22 years (1 year gap for a smaller start up). Matt obtained a BS in Electrical Engineering from Vanderbilt University. Matt resides in Denver with his fiance Dede. For the past 5 years he has been a volunteer basketball coach in the Denver Metro area. Before that he has volunteered at Denver Kids for 5 years to help mentor kids by focusing on school work and other activities.

Earl F. Sison, Creative and Brand Director

Earl moved to San Diego, California in 1997 to escape the cold winters of Ohio and explore the Southern California lifestyle. He started his career in Finance and Marketing after graduating from the University of Dayton in 1996. He soon switched his career and joined the graphic design industry in 2003 to fulfill his creative passion.

Earl joined Thermo Fisher Scientific in 2010, and is currently a Sr. Designer in Digital Virtual Reality (VR) & Augmented Reality (AR) Development. As a team of one, he helps business units craft strategies to create, produce, measure and deploy VR/AR experiences. The use case is for Marketing and each VR/AR experience has his unique point of view with gaming aspects, educational content, and high entertainment “wow” factor.

Earl had gotten involved in Bridges to Manila as he believes the current generation has a responsibility to give back to the Filipino community and carry on the traditions of the culture.

Kate Castro, Board Member

Kate (Mark Castro’s wife) has helped support Bridges to Manila since 2009. She accompanied the family on a trip to the Philippines in 2017, during which she was able to visit Western Visayas State University in Calinog, Iloilo.

Kate worked within the Public Relations industry as a creative producer for over 15 years. She obtained a BFA in Filmmaking at The School of the Art Institute of Chicago. Kate resides in Brookfield, IL with her husband Mark and their three children Ray (8), Myles (7) and Blake (5).

Scott Bentz MD, Board Member

Scott is an Emergency Physician in Denver, Colorado. He graduated from Oberlin College where he earned a degree in Neuroscience. He received his medical degree from Wright State University and completed his Emergency Medicine residency at the University of South Carolina. He has practiced Emergency Medicine since 2002. He served as Emergency Department Medical Director 2009-2020 and Medical Staff President 2016-2018 at Presbyterian/St Luke's Medical Center in Denver, Colorado. He is a member of the Board of Directors for Carepoint PC, the largest democratic physician practice in Colorado and has served in that role from 2009 - present.

He has served on two medical missions to Tanzania with One World Medical Relief. Scott grew up down the street from the Castro family in Amherst, Ohio and has co-hosted two fundraisers for Bridges to Manila in Denver, CO.

Karen Floro, Board Member

Karen is a Board Certified Internal Medicine physician practicing since 2005 with experience as a hospitalist in the hospital, LTAC, acute rehabilitation and post acute care settings. She has an undergraduate degree from Miami University (OH), a DO from Ohio University college of Osteopathic Medicine and did her residency at Akron City Hospital.

As a first generation Filipino in the US, Karen cares deeply about the Philippines and giving back to the community. Her parents are from Bigaa, Bulacan and Catbalogan Samar, Philippines.
